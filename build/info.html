Parameters that majorly affect the water quality

This page provides detailed insights into the key factors that affect water quality, ensuring safe and clean drinking water. The primary focus is on four major parameters:pH Level, Hardness, Turbidity, and Conductivity.

pH Level

Ideal Range:"6.5 - 8.5"
Effect: If pH < 6.5 → Water is acidic →bitter taste, and tooth erosion.
If pH > 8.5 → Water is alkaline → Causes skin irritation, hard water formation.

Hardness

Ideal Range:"0 - 300 mg/L"
Effect: High hardness causes scaling in pipelines, dry skin, and spotted dishes.

Turbidity

Ideal Range:"0 - 5 NTU"
Effect: High turbidity may harbor bacteria, viruses, and contaminants.

Conductivity

Ideal Range: "50 - 500 µS/cm"
Effect: High conductivity indicates excessive dissolved solids.
